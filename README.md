Executive Overview

The UIDAI Decision Intelligence System (UDIS) is an advanced, administrative-grade platform designed to bridge the gap between massive datasets and effective regional governance. Developed for the UIDAI Data Hackathon 2026, UDIS moves beyond reporting dashboards into Advisory Governance Support.

The platform provides explainable decision intelligence for early detection of operational friction, cost-of-inaction forecasting, and region-specific intervention planning — while maintaining strict human authorization, privacy guarantees, and audit-grade accountability.

UDIS is explicitly designed as a decision-support assistant.  
It does not automate policy actions. It augments human judgment.

Core Innovation Pillars

1. Composite Risk Score (CRS)

UDIS abandons univariate thresholds in favor of a Multivariate Composite Index. The CRS is a transparent, explainable metric (0.0 – 1.0) calculated as:

CRS =  
0.35 × Pendency  
+ 0.25 × Delay  
+ 0.25 × Capacity  
+ 0.15 × Volatility

This unified risk index detects systemic regional friction before it manifests as citizen complaints or service failures.

2. Governance Reframing: Cost of Inaction (COI)

UDIS introduces Cost of Inaction logic to project the trajectory of unresolved friction over 14-day and 30-day windows.  
Instead of reporting static status, the system reframes administrative delays into quantified governance risk.

Without calibrated intervention, backlog growth is projected to exceed operational capacity in 3 high-risk clusters within the next 60 days.

3. Explainable Rule Trace (ERT)

Every decision signal generated by UDIS is accompanied by an Explainable Rule Trace.  
This replaces black-box ML with auditable, deterministic logic.

Each alert includes:
• Trigger thresholds  
• Variable contributions  
• Multivariate justification  
• Policy relevance mapping  

This ensures all interventions are defensible under RTI disclosures and parliamentary audits.

4. Predictive Resource Planning

UDIS includes a Seasonal Load Forecast (ARIMA-Lite) to predict enrollment and update volumes for the coming quarter.

This enables pre-emptive redistribution of mobile kits, operator staffing, and biometric camp scheduling for high-friction clusters.

Decision Support vs Automation  
UDIS does not automate policy actions.  
It provides Composite Risk Scores (CRS) and Cost of Inaction projections to support human judgment.

All recommendations generated by UDIS require mandatory manual approval by a verified UIDAI Principal before execution.  
No policy action can be auto-triggered by this system under any circumstances.
